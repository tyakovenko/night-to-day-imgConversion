## 3. Day Target Selection

**Criterion:** `daylight > 0.8` (scores in annotation range [0, 1])

**Rationale:** The `daylight` distribution has a bimodal character — many images cluster near 0 (non-day) and a large cluster near 1.0 (clear daylight). Looking at the histogram, 4,184 images (49%) score above 0.8. The 75th percentile is 0.953, meaning the top quarter of images are essentially all full-daylight. The threshold of 0.8 sits between the 50th percentile (0.791) and 75th percentile (0.953) — it captures the upper ~49% of images by daylight score, all of which are clearly daytime. When multiple images per scene exceed the threshold, the single highest-scoring image is selected to maximize target quality and reduce noise.

## 4. Low-Light Classification

### Attributes Considered

Low-light conditions in natural scenes are not solely captured by the `night` attribute. The following attributes were analyzed:

- **night**: Direct nighttime annotation
- **dark**: General darkness (independent of time of day)
- **dawndusk**: Dawn/dusk transitional lighting (low light, not full night)
- **daylight**: Inverse signal — high daylight excludes images from low-light set
- **bright**: Inverse signal — high brightness contradicts low-light

Additional attributes (fog, storm, glowing, mysterious, gloomy) were examined but found to be weaker discriminators for purely illumination-based low-light classification.

### Final Thresholds

| Attribute | Threshold | Approximate Percentile | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|------------------------|-----------|
| night | > 0.5 | ~p88 (p75=0.166, p90=0.604) | Well above the heavy mass of near-zero scores; captures clearly night images |
| dark | > 0.5 | ~p86 (p75=0.298, p90=0.633) | Well above baseline darkness; captures genuinely dark images |
| dawndusk | > 0.5 | ~p86 (p75=0.295, p90=0.606) | Well above background twilight noise; captures strong transitional low-light |
| daylight | < 0.4 | ~p28 (p25=0.462) | Below the median daylight score; excludes images with meaningful daylight presence |

**Classification rule:** `(night > 0.5 OR dark > 0.5 OR dawndusk > 0.5) AND daylight < 0.4`

### Threshold Justification

The `night`, `dark`, and `dawndusk` distributions are all heavily right-skewed: roughly 65–70% of images score below 0.1 on each attribute (they are primarily daylight scenes). The bulk of the distribution mass sits below 0.3. Setting a threshold of 0.5 means we are selecting only the top ~12–14% of images by these attributes — a deliberately strict cutoff.

This strictness is intentional: the task is to enhance genuinely low-light images. An image with `night=0.2` is likely an overcast day or a scene with some shadow, not a night image. Using a looser threshold (e.g., 0.3) would include a large volume of borderline images that would add noise to training without improving the model's ability to handle true low-light conditions.

### Tradeoff Analysis

| Dimension | Stricter (chosen, threshold=0.5) | Looser (alternative, threshold=0.3) |
|-----------|----------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Low-light purity | Higher — top ~12% of scores | Lower — top ~25% of scores |
| Pair count | ~1,176 pairs | ~2,000+ pairs (estimated) |
| Coverage | 90 of 101 scenes | Near full coverage |
| Training signal quality | Clean, high-contrast pairs | More noise, borderline cases |

**Decision:** Thresholds set near p88 for positive criteria. This errs toward precision — the model should learn from genuinely challenging low-light conditions, not mildly underexposed or transitional images. The `daylight < 0.4` guard further filters out ambiguous images that scored moderate on daylight (the p25 is 0.462, so `< 0.4` is approximately the bottom 28% of daylight scores, meaning these images have very little daylight annotation).

## 9. Open Questions and Ambiguities

1. **Dawn/dusk overlap:** Images with `dawndusk > 0.5` but moderate `dark` scores may represent transitional lighting that is not strictly "night." These are included as a deliberate design choice to increase training diversity, but their visual quality should be reviewed.

2. **Scene coverage gap:** 3 scenes were excluded due to missing day targets. These scenes may contain useful low-light images that could be paired with images from similar scenes if a proxy pairing strategy is adopted in a future iteration.

3. **Confidence weighting:** This analysis uses raw annotation scores without weighting by confidence. High-confidence annotations are more reliable. Future analysis could weight pairs by annotation confidence.

4. **LOL dataset extension:** The Dataset class should be designed to accept the LOL dataset as a second training phase (indoor paired images). The `low_light_manifest.csv` schema is compatible — LOL paths can be appended with `low_light_reason="lol_dataset"`.
